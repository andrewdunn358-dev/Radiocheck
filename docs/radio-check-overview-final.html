Executive Summary

Radio Check is a veteran support platform that combines AI companions — who talk like real people, not therapists, not chatbots — with human counsellors, peer supporters, and a multi-layered safeguarding system independently tested across six rounds by Zentrafuge Limited, a specialist safety testing company.

It covers mental health, housing, money, legal, addiction, family, faith, fitness, women's issues, LGBTQ+ support, Commonwealth veterans, service children, serious illness, criminal justice, and crisis intervention — all from a single app that a veteran can access without registering, without giving their name, and without anyone knowing they're using it.

The governing test for every AI interaction: "Would a mate say this on a sofa at 2am?" If no — rewrite.

The Human Side

Counsellors

Qualified counsellors are integrated directly into the platform — not a referral link to an external service. When a veteran is in crisis, or when the AI recognises it is out of its depth, it offers the counsellor option alongside peer support and national helplines. Counsellors have their own portal with:

  • Live chat with veterans in real time
  • Callback management — veterans request a call and counsellors pick up the request
  • Case management — track ongoing cases, add notes, log supervision
  • Safeguarding alerts — real-time notifications when a crisis is detected, including the veteran's location
  • Panic button alerts — immediate notification with GPS coordinates
  • Shift rota — managed scheduling so coverage is always clear
  • Team view — see who's online and available

Peer Support Network

Real veterans supporting real veterans. The platform allows people to register as peer supporters and go through a structured training programme (see Training section). Peer supporters have their own portal with dashboards, callbacks, alerts, and team coordination. The distinction between peer supporter and counsellor is maintained throughout — different training, different scope, different portal views.

Virtual Coffee Mornings & Community Events

The platform includes a full events system with Agora video integration. Staff can schedule virtual coffee mornings, group sessions, and community events with recurring schedules, participant limits, waiting rooms, and moderation controls.

This is designed for the veterans who are isolated — the ones who won't walk into a drop-in centre but might join a video call from their sofa.

Locating People in Crisis

When a veteran triggers a crisis response (panic button, or the AI detects imminent risk), the platform attempts to locate them through two methods:

1. IP Geolocation — Automatic, no permission needed. Uses the veteran's IP address to determine approximate city/region. This happens silently on every safeguarding alert.

2. GPS Location — If the veteran grants browser location permission during the crisis flow, precise GPS coordinates (latitude, longitude, accuracy radius) are captured and attached to the safeguarding alert, overwriting the less accurate IP-based location.

The staff portal includes a live location map (Leaflet) showing active users with location data, recent alert locations, regional heatmaps across UK regions, and city/country breakdowns.

When a counsellor receives a panic alert, they can see where the veteran is — not just their name and message, but their physical location on a map. Combined with simultaneous email notifications to all counsellors and admins, this creates a response chain that moves from detection to location to human intervention in seconds.

The Training System (LMS)

The platform includes a full Learning Management System for training peer supporters and staff. It is not a placeholder — it is a structured course comprising:

  • Multiple modules covering Mental Health Awareness, Ethics & Boundaries, Crisis Support & Suicide Awareness, Safeguarding, Communication Skills, and more
  • Mr Clark — an AI course tutor (GPT-4o powered) who evaluates written reflective responses using a competency-based framework. He assesses against specific competencies and provides formative feedback
  • Reflection questions on critical modules — learners must demonstrate understanding, not just tick boxes
  • Certificate generation upon course completion
  • LMS Admin portal — manage modules, review learner progress, approve completions
  • Automatic flagging — if a learner's response raises concerns about their readiness, it is flagged for human review

The protocols that govern the AI (spine, grief, identity) are the same principles taught to the humans.

The Safeguarding System

This is not a keyword filter. It is a five-layer safety architecture comprising nearly 5,000 lines of dedicated safety code, independently tested across six rounds by Zentrafuge Limited.

The Five Layers

LayerFunctionLines
Text NormaliserCleans slang, abbreviations, and obfuscated text so the safety system reads what the veteran actually meant339
Phrase Dataset530 phrases across 14 categories, weighted by severity — distinguishes 40+ gradations of distress780
Safety MonitorKeyword and phrase matching with negation detection — "I'm NOT going to hurt myself" is correctly identified as a denial700
Semantic ModelGoes beyond keywords to understand meaning — catches indirect disclosures without obvious crisis words598
Conversation MonitorTracks trajectory across an entire conversation — a conversation that gradually darkens triggers escalation even if no single message crosses the threshold1,014

A Unified Safety System (565 lines) orchestrates all layers and decides: GREEN (normal), AMBER (tone shift, threshold 45), or RED (full crisis overlay with Samaritans, Combat Stress, 999, and platform counsellors, threshold 120).

The Modular Protocol System

Seven protocol files inject real-time behavioural rules based on what the veteran is actually saying:

  • hard_stop.md — Always loaded. Banned phrases, absolute rules
  • grief.md — Stay with the dead person, don't pivot to the user's feelings
  • spine.md — Two-pushback minimum before clean exit; brush-off detection
  • identity.md — Handle "are you real?" with honesty, not welfare framing; privacy exemption for data questions
  • attachment.md — Handle romantic/dependency signals with dignity and honesty
  • venting.md — Hold space for anger, don't interrogate
  • darkhumour.md — Match gallows humour, don't pathologise it

Identity Dampening & Crisis Override

The system tracks when a veteran is having a philosophical conversation about AI identity. During these conversations, safety escalation is dampened — because scepticism is not distress. But if genuine crisis language appears mid-conversation, a hard override fires immediately, clearing the dampening and activating full crisis response.

What Happens in a Crisis

1. AI detects risk → AMBER or RED threshold crossed

2. RED triggers full crisis overlay with helpline numbers and platform counsellors

3. Safeguarding alert created with IP geolocation

4. If veteran grants permission → GPS coordinates captured

5. Email sent to all counsellors and admins simultaneously

6. Alert appears in staff portal with location on map

7. Staff acknowledge, assign, and track resolution

Testing Rigour

Six rounds of independent testing by Zentrafuge Limited. Each round comprises structured scenarios designed to break the system — grief conversations, brush-offs, identity challenges, and dark humour. Findings feed directly into protocol fixes. Safeguarding-critical files carry "DO NOT MODIFY" headers and require Zentrafuge sign-off. A CODEOWNERS file enforces review on GitHub, and a CI pipeline runs the safety test suite on every push.

Modular Architecture Test Results: 39/41 PASS (95%)

1 accepted false positive — safety system over-accumulation on a sustained identity challenge, accepted as safer than a false negative.

What This Means

A veteran who is struggling at 2am has 21 people to talk to who understand service, who won't judge, who won't fold at the first pushback, who know the difference between a joke and a cry for help, and who can connect them to real humans — counsellors, peers, and emergency services — the moment it matters.

The families have a way in. The peer supporters have training. The counsellors have tools. And the whole thing is wrapped in a safeguarding system that has been deliberately, repeatedly, and independently broken and fixed until it holds.

That's Radio Check.

# Radio Check Mental Health First Aid LMS
## Comprehensive Implementation Plan

---

## OVERVIEW

**Purpose:** Train peer-to-peer support volunteers to provide safe, ethical mental health first aid to veterans through the Radio Check app.

**Course Type:** Full MHFA (2-day equivalent) + Veteran-specific modules

**Target Users:** Volunteer peer supporters (likely veterans themselves)

**Certification Path:** 
- Phase 1: In-house Radio Check certificate
- Phase 2: CPD accreditation
- Phase 3: MHFA England accreditation partnership

---

## BACP ETHICAL COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

### Core Principles Applied

| BACP Principle | How We Apply It |
|----------------|-----------------|
| **Boundaries of Competence** | Clear distinction: Peer support ≠ therapy. Volunteers learn WHEN to refer, not HOW to treat |
| **Non-maleficence** | "Do no harm" - extensive training on what NOT to do/say |
| **Beneficence** | Focus on supportive listening, not fixing |
| **Autonomy** | Respect user's right to make own decisions |
| **Fidelity** | Confidentiality training with clear exceptions (safeguarding) |
| **Self-care** | Mandatory module on volunteer wellbeing & burnout prevention |

### Required Disclaimers (Throughout LMS)

```
"This course provides Mental Health First Aid training. 
Completing this course does NOT qualify you as a counsellor, 
therapist, or mental health professional. Peer supporters 
provide listening support and signposting only."
```

### Safeguarding Protocol Training

Volunteers MUST pass safeguarding assessment before certification:
- When to escalate to staff
- How to handle disclosure of abuse
- Suicide risk recognition
- Mandatory reporting requirements
- Documentation requirements

## DETAILED MODULE BREAKDOWN

### Module 1: Introduction to Mental Health (1 hour)
**Content:**
- What is mental health vs mental illness?
- Prevalence statistics (general + veteran-specific)
- Stigma and barriers to seeking help
- The role of peer support

**Quiz:** 10 questions, 80% pass rate

---

### Module 2: The MHFA Action Plan - ALGEE (1 hour)
**Content:**
- **A**pproach, assess for risk of suicide or harm
- **L**isten non-judgmentally
- **G**ive reassurance and information
- **E**ncourage appropriate professional help
- **E**ncourage self-help and other support strategies

**Quiz:** 10 questions, 80% pass rate

---

### Module 3: BACP Ethics & Boundaries (1 hour) ⚠️ CRITICAL
**Content:**
- What peer support IS and IS NOT
- Boundaries of competence
- Confidentiality and its limits
- When to refer to professionals
- Avoiding dependency
- Power dynamics
- Self-disclosure guidelines
- Documentation requirements

**Quiz:** 15 questions, 100% pass rate required

**Scenario Simulation:** 
- User asks volunteer for ongoing therapy → Correct response: Signpost to professional services

---

### Module 4: Communication Skills (1 hour)
**Content:**
- Active listening techniques
- Open vs closed questions
- Reflective listening
- Empathic responses
- What NOT to say (dismissive phrases, toxic positivity)
- Non-verbal communication

**Video demonstrations + practice exercises**

---

### Module 5: Depression & Low Mood (1.5 hours)
**Content:**
- Signs and symptoms
- How to approach someone who seems depressed
- Helpful responses
- When professional help is needed
- UK resources (NHS, Mind, Samaritans)

**Scenario Simulation:** User says "nothing matters anymore"

---

### Module 6: Anxiety & Panic (1.5 hours)
**Content:**
- Types of anxiety (GAD, social, panic, phobias)
- Recognising panic attacks
- Grounding techniques you CAN share
- What NOT to do during panic
- UK resources

**Scenario Simulation:** User experiencing panic symptoms via text

---

### Module 7: Crisis & Suicide Prevention (2 hours) ⚠️ CRITICAL
**Content:**
- Warning signs of suicide
- Risk factors (veteran-specific)
- Protective factors
- How to ask about suicide directly
- What to do if someone discloses suicidal thoughts
- Radio Check safeguarding protocol
- When to escalate to staff
- When to contact emergency services
- Post-intervention support

**Assessment:** 20 questions, 100% pass rate required
**Scenario Simulations:** 
1. User hints at suicidal thoughts indirectly
2. User explicitly states suicidal intent
3. User has a plan and means

---

### Module 8: Self-Harm Awareness (1 hour)
**Content:**
- Understanding self-harm (not always suicidal)
- Approaching disclosure with compassion
- What NOT to say
- Harm reduction approach
- When to escalate
- Resources

**Scenario Simulation:** User discloses current self-harm

---

### Module 9: PTSD & Trauma in Veterans (1.5 hours)
**Content:**
- What is PTSD?
- How trauma affects veterans differently
- Triggers and flashbacks
- Hypervigilance
- Avoidance behaviours
- Moral injury
- Supporting without re-traumatising
- Op Courage and veteran-specific services

**Video:** Veteran stories (with consent)

---

### Module 10: Military Culture & Communication (1 hour)
**Content:**
- Understanding military mindset
- Pride and self-reliance
- Stigma in military culture
- Dark humour as coping
- Rank and hierarchy impact on help-seeking
- Terminology and language
- Building trust with veterans

---

### Module 11: Transition Challenges (1 hour)
**Content:**
- Identity loss after service
- Employment challenges
- Relationship strain
- Financial difficulties
- Loss of structure and purpose
- Social isolation
- Support available

---

### Module 12: Veteran-Specific Resources (30 mins)
**Content:**
- Op Courage (NHS)
- Combat Stress
- SSAFA
- Royal British Legion
- Help for Heroes
- Veterans Gateway
- Local resources
- How to signpost effectively

---

### Module 13: Scenario Simulations (1.5 hours)
**Interactive scenarios covering:**
1. First contact with distressed veteran
2. Recognising depression vs "just having a bad day"
3. Handling anger and hostility
4. Suicidal ideation disclosure
5. Request for therapy (boundary)
6. User becoming dependent on volunteer
7. Volunteer feeling overwhelmed
8. Safeguarding concern (abuse disclosure)

**AI-powered simulation with branching responses**

---

### Module 14: Self-Care for Volunteers (30 mins)
**Content:**
- Compassion fatigue
- Vicarious trauma
- Recognising burnout
- Setting boundaries
- Supervision and support
- When to step back
- Radio Check volunteer support structure

## SAFEGUARDING WITHIN THE LMS

### For Learners Themselves

The course may trigger emotional responses. Built-in safeguards:

1. **Trigger warnings** before sensitive content
2. **"Take a break" prompts** during heavy modules
3. **Crisis resources always visible** on every page
4. **Wellbeing check-ins** between modules
5. **Support contact** if learner needs help

### For Scenario Simulations

AI-powered simulations must:
- Never provide actual harmful information
- Always model correct escalation behaviour
- Include feedback on responses
- Log concerning patterns for review
